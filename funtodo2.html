6.Piggy Bank




Waste Required

  • Bottle
  • Coloured paper
  • Parmanent marker
  • Glue
  • 4 extra bottle tops
  • Eyes

Steps

  1. With scissors cut a strip of pink craft paper to surround the middle section of the bottle.
  2. Mark a small coin shaped rectangle in the centre of the strip.Stick the card to the bottle.
  3. Ask an adult to cut around the rectangle to make a coin slot in the bottle and card.
  4. Paint the lid of the bottle pink,once the lid is dry draw two nostrils on the lid with a marker pen.
  5. Stick or draw two eyes on the front of the bottle,draw and cut out two ear shapes from the pink card.
  6. Draw and cut out a long thin strip of pink card – twist to create a pig’s curly tail,attach the ears and tail.
  7. Stick the remaining bottle lids on the base of the bottle to make legs.Enjoy your creation!


7.Photoframe Using Icecream Stick




Waste Required

  • Icecream Stick
  • Scissor
  • Glue
  • Paints
  • Crayons
  • Paper

Steps

  1. Take Icecrem sticks clean it and then paint it with atleast two different colors
  2. Place 2 sticks parallel.Glue 2 more sticks by the flat edges and wait till dry.
  3. Do the same for the layer you just put on and wait to dry.
  4. Turn the upright stick to the base wait for it to dry
  5. Stick decorations on the sticks wait for to dry,Take some glue and put it on the back.
  6. Take the last stick and place it in the glue at an angle that will allow the frame to stay still when the frame is balanced on the stick
  7. Finished! Now glue the picture to the back of the frame.


8.Bookmark Using Icecream Stick




Waste Required

  • Icecream Stick
  • Felt Eyes
  • Glue
  • Paints/Sketch Pen

Steps

  1. Take Icecream stick clean it properly
  2. With your yellow paper turned landscape, draw a line from the top to the bottom about an inch in from the right hand edge. Then draw an inch wide circle and several inch-tall petal shapes with straight bottom edges and tops shaped like a hearts. Cut out these red shapes and your yellow strips. I cut about eight petals in total
  3. Turn your yellow strip so it is longer horizontally and fold it in half, so it’s only half as tall as it was before. Along the full length of your yellow strip on the creased side, make small cuts from the bottom edge upwards, only about a quarter of the way towards the top where the edges meet. This creates a sort of fringed effect.
  4. Turn your yellow strip vertically and apply some glue on one side at the short bottom edge. Starting rolling the edge in on itself and continue rolling it upwards. Apply glue at the other end and stick it down when your yellow roll meets it. Set this aside for now.
  5. Build the base of your flower and its petals by applying glue to the backs of the petal pieces near the straight bottom edges and placing them around the outside of the red circle so they overlap slightly in layers.
  6. Apply glue on the rolled side of your yellow piece, on the bottom underneath your fringed cuts. Stick it flat side down in the center of your red flower, where all the straight petal edges overlap. Feel free to bend your fringed yellow center back a little to make it look more splayed and voluminous.
  7. Cut a strip from your ribbon that is the same length as your popsicle stick. Next, apply glue all along its length on one flat side and stick the ribbon down flat, smoothing down any creases.
  8. Turn your ribbon into a little bow! Cut a piece about four inches long and apply some glue in the middle along its length. Take each end and curve it inwards, towards the middle, to create a looped shape, letting an inch or so of the ribbon’s end hang below the loop. Stick that piece in the glue you applied in the center and then repeat the process with the other side.
  9. Apply glue to the back of your red flower and stick it to one end of your ribboned popsicle stick. This is not the top of your flower!
  10. Apply glue to the back of your bow, in the center of its back side, and stick if below the yellow center of your flower, resting on the red petals.


9.Plane Using Icecream Stick




Waste Required

  • Icecream Stick
  • 1 wooden clothespin
  • Glue
  • Paints
  • Scissors/Cutter
  • Eraser/bread

Steps

  1. Wash and dry the ice cream sticks.
  2. Paint the sticks as well as the clothespin in any colour you please and let them dry
  3. Cut an ice cream stick into 3 pieces and join two end pieces in a straight line with glue. This is to make the plane’s propeller.
  4. Cut a tiny little bit of the eraser, paint it and leave it aside to dry or you can simply use a small bead.
  5. Glue 2 ice cream sticks to the clothespin, perpendicular and equidistant from each other
  6. Flip to the other side of the clip and repeat the same process with 2 more ice cream sticks, so that they overlap each other
  7. Glue the eraser or bead to the ice cream stick so that the propeller is complete
  8. Finally, stick the propeller on the mouth of the clothespin. Your plane is ready


10.Tin Can Lanterns




Waste Required

  • Tin can
  • Steel Wire
  • Nail
  • Hammer/Pliers
  • Lights
  • Paint

Steps

  1. Wash and clean the tin can and let it dry.
  2. Fill the can with water and put it in the freezer for some time
  3. Remove the can from the freezer and then poke two holes in the can, one on each side.
  4. Use the wire to make a handle attaching it to the two holes
  5. Make various holes around the can with hammer and nails. You can choose to create a pattern of holes or just be random.
  6. Paint the inside and outside of the cans in fun, vibrant colours.
  7. Once it’s all dry, you can put fairy lights or tea light candles in them to turn them into lanterns.
